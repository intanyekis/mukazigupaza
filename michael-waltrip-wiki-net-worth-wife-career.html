Where was Michael Waltrip born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

He approached his father and older brother Darell Waltrip for help when he was a youngster in order to advance his racing abilities. But they did give him the go-ahead to focus on his academics. At that point, his brother Bobby helped him stay motivated by going with him to the go-kart races in Kentucky.

In 1993, Waltrip wed Elizabeth Buffy Franks. They have a beautiful daughter together, however, they got divorced in 2010. In addition, he has a child from a prior union.

How did Michael Waltrip start his Professional Career?

When Michael Waltrip was 15 years old, his brother Bobby accompanied him to a small go-kart race in their Kentucky hometown. This is when Michael Waltrip started competing. When he participated in the Kentucky Motor Speedway’s Mini-Modified track championship that year, his professional stock-car racing career really got off.
In 1983, he took home the Darlington Dash Series Championship. He participated in the Winston Open in 1996 and took first place. Later, he entered the Sprint Cup Series of NASCAR.

From 1987 to 1995, Waltrip was employed by Bahari Racing, followed by Wood Brothers Racing from 1996 to 1998, then Jimmy Smith/ Mattei Racing from 1999 to 2000.

Before quitting in 2005, he won four team races while working for “Dale Earnhardt Inc.” In the 2004 NASCAR Xfinity Series, he took first place. Waltrip operated the No. 55 vehicle in the NASCAR Spirit Cup series in 2007.

He took part in the NASCAR Camping World Truck Series in 2004 and placed 61st. He was last seen competing in the Daytona 500 in his Toyota Camry for Premium Motorsports. Waltrip declared that he will drive the No. 15 Aaron’s, Inc. Camry for Premium Motorsports and compete in the Daytona 500.
