Barry Manilow ethnicity, Wikipedia, Children, Daughter, Age, Young – Barry Manilow is an American icon known for his incredible talents in singing, songwriting, and more. Let’s delve into the life of this versatile artist, exploring his career, personal life, and noteworthy achievements.

Barry Manilow Early Life

Barry Alan Pincus, born to Edna Manilow and Harold Pincus, grew up in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. Raised in a Jewish household with Irish-American ancestry, he attended Eastern District High School, graduating in 1961. Eager to pursue his passion for the arts, he applied to the Juilliard Performing Arts School.

Facing the financial challenges of education, Barry took a job at CBS while attending Juilliard. His journey took an unexpected turn when CBS director Bro Herrod noticed his musical talent and asked him to organize songs for a musical adaptation of ‘The Drunkard.’ Barry composed the entire score, marking the beginning of his musical career.

Barry Manilow Career Beginnings

In 1967, Barry became the musical director for the CBS network television show. He gained recognition by creating jingles for popular commercials like ‘Band-Aid’ and ‘Dr. Pepper.’ Collaborating with Bette Midler in the early ’70s, he contributed to her albums ‘The Divine Miss M’ and ‘Bette Midler.’

Barry’s solo career took off in 1973 with the release of his debut album, ‘Barry Manilow.’ This was followed by ‘Barry Manilow II’ in the same year. His third album, ‘Tryin’ to Get the Feeling,’ released in 1975, showcased his evolving musical prowess.

Barry Manilow Television Specials and Hit Albums

Entering the late ’70s, Barry expanded his influence by producing and starring in four television specials for ABC, including ‘The Barry Manilow Special.’ The release of his fifth studio album, ‘Even Now’ in 1978, marked a significant milestone, reaching triple platinum status.

Barry’s presence extended to the big screen when his song ‘Ready to Take a Chance Again’ featured in the film ‘Foul Play,’ earning an Oscar nomination for ‘Best Original Score’ in 1978. The subsequent years saw a flurry of activity, with successful albums like ‘Manilow Magic’ and ‘One Voice.’

The ’80s brought more achievements, including a sold-out show at Wembley Stadium and the release of albums like ‘Barry’ and ‘If I Should Love Again.’ His live album, ‘Barry Live in Britain,’ recorded at the Royal Albert Hall in 1982, achieved platinum status.

Ventures into New Avenues

Barry Manilow’s career wasn’t limited to music. In 1984, he released ‘2:00 AM Paradise Café,’ recorded in a single take. The following year, his self-titled album ‘Manilow’ coincided with his role as ‘Tony Star’ in the CBS show ‘Copacabana.’

Adding author to his list of accomplishments, Barry published his autobiography ‘Sweet Life: Adventures on the Way to Paradise’ in 1987. This period also saw him signing with Arista Records and releasing the album ‘Swing Street.’

The late ’80s and early ’90s showcased Barry’s continued creativity with albums like ‘Barry Manilow,’ ‘Because It’s Christmas,’ and a string of ‘event’ albums. He ventured into Broadway musical scores for ‘Thumbelina’ and ‘The Pebble and the Penguin.’

New Millennium Ventures

Entering the new millennium, Barry Manilow remained a force to be reckoned with. Notable releases included ‘Ultimate Manilow,’ ‘2 Nights Live!,’ and ‘The Greatest Songs of the Fifties.’ His concert recorded for PBS, ‘Barry Manilow: Music and Passion,’ earned him an Emmy Award in 2006.

The following years brought more albums, such as ‘The Greatest Songs of the Sixties’ and ‘Barry Manilow: Songs from the Seventies.’ In 2011, he hosted the documentary series ‘They Write the Songs’ for BBC Radio 2. The album ’15 Minutes’ marked another chapter in his enduring career.

Barry continued to captivate audiences with live performances, including appearances at significant events like ‘A Capitol Forth’ and ‘Children in Need Rocks 2013.’

Barry Manilow Major Works and Achievements

In 1974, Barry’s second album, ‘Barry Manilow II,’ featured the breakthrough hit ‘Mandy,’ propelling him to stardom. The album achieved 2X Platinum status and secured the ninth position on US countdowns. His 1975 release, ‘Tryin’ to Get the Feeling,’ earned triple platinum in the United States, solidifying his musical legacy.

Barry’s accolades include an Emmy Award in 1977 for ‘Outstanding Special – Comedy, Variety or Music’ for ‘The Barry Manilow Special.’ In 1978, he clinched the American Music Award for ‘Favorite Pop/Rock Male Artist’ and the Grammy Award in 1979 for ‘Best Pop Male Vocal Performance’ for ‘Copacabana.’

Inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 2002, Barry’s contributions to the music industry have been monumental. In 2006, he won another Emmy Award for ‘Outstanding Individual Performance in a Variety or Music Program’ for ‘Barry Manilow: Music and Passion.’ His record-breaking sales of 75 million earned him an RIAA Plaque, and he received an honorary Clio Award for his early work with jingles.

Barry Manilow Personal Life and Legacy

In 1964, Barry married Susan Deixler, but the marriage was annulled the following year. Over the years, he faced health challenges, including a sprained and fractured ankle, bronchial pneumonia, non-cancerous cyst in his upper jaw, eye surgery, and two hip surgeries.

Despite rumors of an affair with pornographic actress Robin Byrd, Barry has largely kept his personal life private. His legacy extends beyond music, encompassing his influence in various entertainment realms.
