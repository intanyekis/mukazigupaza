The Meaning Behind The Song: Circles by Soul Coughing

June 2024 · 3 minute read

I am currently listening to “Circles” by Soul Coughing as I write this article. This song holds a special place in my heart and brings back memories of my teenage years. It has an addictive groove and catchy lyrics that stick with you long after the song ends. But what is the meaning behind this infectious track?

“Circles” was written by Sebastian Steinberg, Yuval Gabay, Mark Degliantoni, and Mike Doughty. It was released as a single from their album “El Oso” in 1998. The song quickly gained popularity, becoming one of Soul Coughing’s most successful songs to date.

The lyrics of “Circles” explore themes of monotony, frustration, and the longing for something more. It starts with the lines, “When you were languishing in rooms I built to file you in, and when the wind set down in funnel form and pulled you in.” These opening lines paint a picture of someone trapped in a confined space, feeling stuck and unable to escape.

Throughout the song, there is a recurring refrain: “I don’t need to walk around in circles, walk around in circles, walk around in circles, walk around in… Don’t need to walk around in circles, walk around in circles, walk around in circles, walk around in.” This repetition emphasizes the frustration and desire for change.

The second verse introduces the theme of violence, with the lines, “And when the ghostly dust of violence traces everything, and when the gas runs out just wreck it, you insured the thing.” Here, the lyrics suggest a destructive cycle that repeats itself, leaving the protagonist feeling trapped and hopeless.

The bridge section of the song adds an interesting twist to the meaning. It includes the repeated lines, “Doom da doom da doom, well I’m going, I’m going.” These lines bring a sense of defiance and determination. It signals a resolve to break free from the circles and monotony.

As the song progresses, it becomes apparent that the protagonist has made a decision to move forward. The lyrics state, “But I can’t sigh now that you made the move, it has gone and gone to dogs, lay down on the floor. For the right price, I can get everything, slip into the car, go driving to the farthest star.” These lines suggest a sense of liberation and a willingness to venture into the unknown.

Soul Coughing’s “Circles” captures the frustration of feeling trapped and stuck in a monotonous routine. It is a song that resonates with anyone who longs for change and seeks to break free from the cycles that hold them back. The repetitive nature of the lyrics amplifies this message, while the bridge provides a glimmer of hope and determination.

Personally, “Circles” holds a nostalgic place in my heart. It reminds me of my teenage years, when I often found myself yearning for something more in life. Whenever I listen to this song, it serves as a reminder to break free from the comfortable yet stagnant routines and pursue my dreams.

So next time you find yourself feeling trapped and frustrated, give “Circles” a listen. Let the infectious groove and meaningful lyrics remind you that you have the power to break free from the circles and create your own path.
