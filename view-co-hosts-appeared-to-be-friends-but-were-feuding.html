These View Co-Hosts Appeared To Be Friends But Were Really Feuding The Whole Time

June 2024 · 7 minute read

Highlights

  • The hosts of The View often find themselves embroiled in controversies and facing backlash for expressing differing opinions.
  • Secret feuds and heated arguments occur behind the scenes of The View, with Alyssa Farah Griffin and Sunny Hostin clashing.
  • The View has a history of on-screen debates getting personal behind the scenes, with instances involving Elisabeth Hasselbeck, Joy Behar and Barbara Walters.

The hosts of The View aren't afraid to express differing opinions when the occasion calls for it. As part of their roles on the daytime series, the co-hosts share their perspectives on current events but often find themselves embroiled in controversies. This includes Sunny Hostin facing backlash after name-dropping Kim Kardashian.

Despite their composed demeanor on-screen, some of The View co-hosts are secretly feuding behind the scenes, while others have come close to walking out of the set during heated arguments.

8 Are Whoopi Goldberg And Sunny Hostin Secretly Feuding Despite Appearing To Be Friends?

Date Aired - March 30, 2023

During a discussion on Artificial Intelligence on The View, co-hosts Whoopi Goldberg and Sunny Hostin visibly struggled to conceal their heated exchange. Their disagreement started when Hostin suggested that Tesla cars are a form of AI, which was immediately dismissed by Goldberg. Despite Hostin's attempts to explain her perspective, Goldberg interrupted her, and they continued to clash until co-host Sara Haines redirected the conversation.

6 Elisabeth Hasselback And Barbara Walters Clashed Over Morning-After-Pill

Date Aired - 2006

According to Page Six, during a heated debate about the morning-after pill with Barbara Walters, Elisabeth Hasselbeck was so frustrated that she contemplated walking off the set in the middle of the taping.

Hasselbeck allegedly expressed to Joy Behar her frustration:

"What the f---! I don't even swear. She has me swearing. This woman is driving me nuts. I'm not going back. I can't do the show like this. She just reprimanded me, and she knew exactly what she was doing. Goodbye! I'm off. Read about that in the New York f---- Post!"

Hasselbeck even went so far as to threaten to quit the show permanently, although she ultimately reconsidered her decision.

3 Rosie O'Donnell Accused Whoopi Goldberg Of Being Mean To Her On And Off The Screen

Date Aired - September 29, 2009

Rosie O'Donnell and Whoopi Goldberg's dispute started in 2009 after O'Donnell disagreed when Goldberg defended Roman Polanski over his sexual assault allegations. When O'Donnell returned to The View in 2014, Goldberg allegedly gave her a chilly reception both on and off-camera.

In her reflections on Ramin Setoodeh's book, O'Donnell addressed the tension with Goldberg and stated that Goldberg was exceptionally harsh towards her on live television.

"Whoopi Goldberg was as mean as anyone has ever been on television to me, personally—while I was sitting there. Worse than Fox News. The worst experience I've ever had on live television was interacting with her."

2 Jenny McCarthy Was Terrified Of Barbara Walters

Date Aired - September 3, 2007

Jenny McCarthy shared contrasting experiences with Barbara Walters during her appearances on The View. In 2007, McCarthy appeared as a guest on the show to discuss her book about autism, and she recounted a heated encounter with Walters, who vehemently reprimanded McCarthy's views on autism being curable.

Upon joining The View in 2013, McCarthy was criticized for her anti-vaccine stance and Walters repeatedly clashed with McCarthy. McCarthy told Ramin Setoodeh that Walters demanded changes to her outfits and scolded her over incidents like a misplaced tampon, leading McCarthy to feel restricted and unhappy.

"I was going to work crying. I couldn't be myself. My fans were telling me, 'Where's Jenny? They aren't letting you be you.'"

McCarthy expressed sadness witnessing Walters' requests to moderate being denied by Goldberg. Despite recognizing Walters' groundbreaking contributions to female journalism, Goldberg resisted giving up the role.
